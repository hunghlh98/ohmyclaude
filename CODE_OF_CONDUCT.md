# Code of Conduct

## Our Standard

This project exists to make Claude Code more powerful for engineers. Contributions are judged on their technical merit and whether they improve the tool for users.

We expect contributors to:

- Be direct and specific in feedback — "this hook has a race condition at line 42" not "bad code"
- Accept that plans get challenged (that's what Nemesis and Eris are for — apply the same standard to PRs)
- Separate the idea from the person — critique the implementation, not the contributor
- Follow the working style in [CONTRIBUTING.md](CONTRIBUTING.md)

We do not tolerate:

- Harassment, personal attacks, or discriminatory language
- Deliberate scope creep ("while we're here, let's also...")
- Committing directly to `main`
- Breaking the validator without fixing it

## Reporting

If something is wrong, open an issue. Be specific about what happened, what you expected, and what context is needed to reproduce it.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may close issues, reject PRs, or remove contributors who repeatedly ignore these standards. No appeals process — contribute in good faith and it won't come up.

---

This code of conduct applies to all project spaces: issues, PRs, discussions, and any external representation of this project.
